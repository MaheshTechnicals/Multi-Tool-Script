Physics Behind Speed Calculations

The Fundamental Relationship

Speed, distance, and time are related by the fundamental equation: Speed = Distance รท Time. This relationship forms the basis of kinematics and is essential for understanding motion in physics.

Average vs. Instantaneous Speed

Our calculator computes average speed over a given distance and time. Instantaneous speed (velocity at a specific moment) requires calculus and is different from average speed in accelerating motion.

Units and Dimensional Analysis

Speed units are always distance units divided by time units (km/h, m/s, mph). Understanding dimensional analysis helps verify calculation correctness and unit conversions.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between speed and velocity?

Speed is a scalar quantity (magnitude only) while velocity is a vector (magnitude and direction). Our calculator computes speed - the rate of distance covered over time.

How accurate are the speed calculations?

Our calculator provides high precision results with up to 6 decimal places. For most practical applications, this exceeds the accuracy of real-world measurements.

Can I calculate acceleration with this tool?

This tool calculates constant speed scenarios. For acceleration calculations (change in velocity over time), you would need a specialized kinematics calculator.

Which speed units are most commonly used?

km/h and mph are common for vehicles, m/s is the SI standard for scientific work, and ft/s is used in some engineering applications. Choose based on your specific needs.
