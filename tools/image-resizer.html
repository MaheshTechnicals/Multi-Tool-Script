Image Resizing Best Practices

Scaling Down vs. Scaling Up

Scaling Down: Generally produces excellent results with minimal quality loss. Perfect for web optimization and file size reduction.

Scaling Up: Use sparingly and avoid extreme enlargements. Our algorithms minimize pixelation, but original resolution limits apply.

Choosing the Right Dimensions

• Consider your final use case (web, print, social media)
• Maintain aspect ratio for natural-looking results
• Use platform-specific presets for social media
• Test different sizes to find the optimal balance

Frequently Asked Questions

What image formats can I resize?

Our image resizer supports JPG, PNG, and WebP formats. These cover virtually all common image types used on the web and in digital media.

Will resizing affect image quality?

Scaling down typically maintains excellent quality. Scaling up may introduce some softness, but our advanced algorithms minimize quality loss compared to basic resizing tools.

Can I resize images for print?

Yes! Set custom dimensions in pixels and ensure your source image has sufficient resolution (typically 300 DPI for print quality).

How do I maintain image proportions?

Use the aspect ratio lock feature (chain link icon) to automatically adjust height when you change width, or vice versa, maintaining natural proportions.
