Password Length and Security

8-12 Characters

Basic Security: Suitable for low-risk accounts but vulnerable to advanced attacks. Minimum recommended length for most services.

12-16 Characters

Good Security: Recommended for most personal accounts including email, social media, and online shopping. Provides good protection against common attacks.

16-24 Characters

Strong Security: Ideal for financial accounts, work systems, and sensitive data. Extremely difficult to crack even with advanced computing power.

24+ Characters

Maximum Security: Perfect for high-value targets, administrative accounts, and long-term storage. Virtually unbreakable with current technology.

Best Practices for Password Security

  • Use Unique Passwords: Never reuse passwords across multiple accounts. Each account should have its own unique password.
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication: Combine strong passwords with 2FA for an additional layer of security.
  • Use a Password Manager: Store generated passwords securely in a reputable password manager application.
  • Regular Updates: Change passwords regularly, especially for sensitive accounts and after security breaches.
  • Avoid Personal Information: Don't include names, birthdays, or other personal information that could be guessed or found online.
  • Check for Breaches: Regularly check if your accounts have been compromised in data breaches.

Common Password Attacks and Protection

Brute Force Attacks

Attackers try every possible combination. Our long, complex passwords make these attacks computationally infeasible, requiring millions of years to crack.

Dictionary Attacks

Using common words and phrases. Our random character generation ensures passwords don't appear in any dictionary or common password list.

Social Engineering

Guessing based on personal information. Random passwords provide no clues about the user's identity, interests, or personal details.

Credential Stuffing

Using leaked passwords from other breaches. Unique passwords ensure that a breach on one site doesn't compromise other accounts.

Frequently Asked Questions

How secure are the generated passwords?

Our passwords are generated using cryptographically secure random number generation, making them extremely secure. A 12-character password with mixed characters would take billions of years to crack with current technology.

Are my passwords stored or transmitted?

No, all password generation happens locally in your browser. We don't store, log, or transmit any passwords. Your generated passwords are completely private and secure.

What length should I choose for my passwords?

We recommend at least 12 characters for most accounts, 16+ for sensitive accounts like banking, and 20+ for high-security applications. Longer passwords are exponentially more secure.

Should I include special characters?

Yes, including special characters significantly increases password strength by expanding the character pool. However, check if your target service supports all special characters before using them.

How often should I change my passwords?

Change passwords immediately if you suspect a breach, every 3-6 months for sensitive accounts, and annually for less critical accounts. Focus on using unique, strong passwords rather than frequent changes.

Can I use these passwords for all my accounts?

You should use a unique password for each account. Generate a separate password for every service to ensure that a breach on one site doesn't compromise your other accounts.
